Policy Summary

The Global Carbon Reward prices systemic risk through three reward channels (CDR, conventional mitigation, and co-benefits) while indexing payouts to the Risk Cost of Carbon (RCC) described in Chen (2025).

What is it?

  • The Global Carbon Reward (XCR) is a carbon-linked, sovereign-backed asset issued as conditional grants for verified removals, reductions, and co-benefits across Reward Channels 1–3.
  • A Carbon Exchange Authority (CEA) sets RCC price paths, manages 100-year durability contracts, milestones, and clawbacks, and administers the reward channels.
  • A rolling XCR price floor, aligned with the mitigation roadmap, anchors long-term value so capital reallocates at the scale of systemic risk.

Key Features

  • Reward Channels cover carbon dioxide removal, conventional mitigation, and community/ecosystem dividends without carbon offsetting.
  • An alliance of central banks guarantees the XCR price floor via Carbon Quantitative Easing (CQE), enabling the reallocation of over US$3 trillion per year toward mitigation.
  • The policy establishes XCR as a limited-risk financial asset for investors and imposes no direct fiscal costs on governments or firms.
  • Implementation begins with voluntary climate clubs and scales through treaty pathways that respect national sovereignty.

Why is it Good Policy?

  • Pairs the polluter-pays principle with the preventive insurance principle to manage systemic risk to the carbon cycle.
  • Stabilizes financial markets by using CQE only when XCR spot prices threaten the price floor.
  • Requires science-based RCC targets and 100-year durability contracts.
  • Channels Reward Channel 3 dividends to communities and ecosystems, reducing greenwashing relative to offset markets.

Summarized from Chen (2025), Executive Summary §§5–7 and Sections 5.7 & 8.3.

Integrated Carbon Pricing Stack

  1. Carbon Exchange Authority (CEA) issues XCR, enforces RCC price guidance, and manages Reward Channels 1–3 with long-duration contracts.
  2. Central Bank Alliance intervenes with Carbon Quantitative Easing (CQE) to defend the rolling XCR price floor in FX markets.
  3. Mitigation Providers deliver verified outcomes across energy, industry, land, oceans, and social systems with milestone-based MRV.
  4. Communities & Ecosystems receive Reward Channel 3 dividends ranked by wellbeing and resilience indicators.

Why policy leaders care

  • Systemic risk lens: complements polluter-pays with preventative insurance economics.
  • Macro stability: integrates climate mandates into monetary policy toolkits.
  • Voluntary & Sovereign: each nation selects scale, governance, and verification partners.
  • Transparent MRV: satellite, IoT, and community-led monitoring feed a public rewards registry.

Dirty Growth

The world economy, as it functions today, must grow to remain stable. However, this growth is unsustainable because it is producing excessive greenhouse gas emissions, global heating, and ecosystem breakdown. How can we resolve this conundrum?

Carbon-Balanced A-Growth

A resolution to the conundrum of dirty economic growth is presented in the 2025 working paper on the carbon reward policy. The carbon reward policy, in combination with other market and non-market policies, will establish what is called 'carbon-balanced agnostic-growth' or more simply, 'carbon-balanced a-growth.'

See Chen (2025), Section 8.4 for the full description of carbon-balanced agnostic-growth.

Demonstrations in development

Clean baseload swaps

Converting coal-heavy grids to renewables + storage with reward-backed cash flow support.

  • Scale: 8-12 GW
  • Reward ask: $6B equivalent

North America

Industrial Carbon Removal

Portfolio of DAC + biomass projects with community benefit scoring and MRV stack.

  • Partners: Tech & tribal authorities
  • Scale: 8 MtCO₂e/year
  • Reward ask: $4B equivalent

SE Asia

Blue carbon corridors

Restoring mangroves and fisheries while creating sovereign revenue-sharing models.

  • Partners: Coastal governments + NGOs
  • Scale: 2M hectares
  • Reward ask: $1.2B equivalent

Europe

Climate-safe buildings

Retrofitting social housing with reward-backed KPIs tied to health and energy savings.

  • Partners: Urban developers, health agencies
  • Scale: 90k units
  • Reward ask: $2B equivalent
