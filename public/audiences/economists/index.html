For Economists

Carbon pricing theory, macroeconomic impacts, and central bank mechanisms

Global Carbon Reward for Economists

The carbon reward introduces a systemic approach to pricing risk that blends monetary policy, preventive insurance, and carbon-balanced growth. Explore the core concepts and macro signals below.

Economic Framework

Framework

Systemic Externality Lens

Extends carbon pricing beyond Pigouvian taxes to address lock-in, tipping points, and financial contagion.

  • Separates social efficiency pricing from systemic safety pricing
  • Introduces Class 1‚Äì4 risk taxonomy to guide policy selection
  • Frames climate as market-and-system failure

Framework

Preventive Insurance Principle

Pairs polluter-pays ('stick') with carbon rewards ('carrot') to insure against systemic damages.

  • XCR priced via the Risk Cost of Carbon (RCC) path and shadow price
  • Rewards operate as a preventive insurance premium
  • Aligns central bank mandates with climate stabilization

Framework

Carbon-Balanced Agnostic Growth

Supports economic stability without fossil expansion by balancing conventional, carbon-negative, and fully electrified sectors.

  • Encourages structural shifts rather than forced degrowth
  • Integrates with Van den Bergh‚Äôs agnostic growth concept
  • Links monetary design to long-run productivity

Framework

Carbon Exchange Authority

Introduces a quasi-fiscal institution overseeing reward issuance, MRV, and 100-year durability contracts.

  • Administers Reward Channels 1‚Äì3
  • Coordinates with central banks via CQE
  • Manages milestones, clawbacks, and co-benefit scoring

Macroeconomic Implications

Macroeconomic Impact

Capital Reallocation

Redirects trillions from fossil assets to clean infrastructure by guaranteeing XCR revenue streams.

  • Reward Channels finance energy, industry, and land systems
  • Private ownership targets (>60%) keep inflation tolerable
  • Aligns with macroprudential goals for orderly transition

Macroeconomic Impact

Financial Stability

Integrates climate risk into monetary mandates to avoid disorderly repricing.

  • CQE only activates during low-demand periods
  • XCR acts as a safe-haven asset during turbulence
  • Reduces stranded asset risk across banking systems

Macroeconomic Impact

International Cooperation

Resolves cost-sharing disputes via voluntary market participation and GDP/emissions contributions.

  • Climate clubs demonstrate readiness before treaty adoption
  • Reward funding accessible to all nations with verified mitigation
  • Transparent MRV and clawbacks maintain trust

Macroeconomic Impact

Growth Model Transformation

Supports carbon-balanced agnostic growth with measurable wellbeing co-benefits.

  • Reward Channel 3 funds community and ecosystem dividends
  • Enables social equity metrics alongside mitigation
  • Encourages innovation without imposing new taxes
