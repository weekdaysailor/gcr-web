Redefining Climate Economics with a Carbon Currency

Redefining Climate Economics with a Carbon Currency

Event: Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences Presentation Date: November 24, 2023, 11:00 AM CET Location: Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences, Centre for Economic Transformation, HvA Corry Tendeloohuis, Fraijlemaborg 133, 1102 CV Amsterdam

Description

The presentation explored an innovative economic theory addressing systemic climate risk. As described, the session examined “why central banks are needed to support a new policy for climate mitigation” through a novel financial instrument called carbon currency.

Key Topics Covered

The carbon currency framework targets four primary applications:

  1. Exchanging fossil fuel reserves for clean energy assets
  2. Decarbonizing carbon-intensive industries
  3. Atmospheric carbon removal
  4. Supporting community and ecosystem co-benefits

The presenter critiqued conventional economic models, arguing they inadequately address systemic market failures in carbon pricing.

Participants

  • Presenter: Dr. Delton Chen (Project Director & Founder, GCR)
  • Moderator: Dr. Willem van Winden (Professor of Urban Economic Studies)
